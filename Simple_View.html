    This is the default behavior of the application. And can be used whenever your screen is oriented vertically.

   

    By default you can convert from Roman numerals to Arabic numerals (left image). For start conversion just press the symbol you want and the corresponding Arabic numeral will consequently appear. When a pressing a symbol can result in an invalid Roman numeral, that symbol is painted in a different, like blurred, style.

   

    On the other hand if what you want to do is convert from Arabic numeral, to Roman numerals (right image), just click the button in the bottom of the screen and the application will automatically switch conversion mode.

   

    When converting from Arabic numerals to Roman numerals, just click the number you want, and the corresponding Roman numeral will consequently appear. As before, when pressing a number can result in an invalid Roman numeral, that symbol is painted in a different, like blurred style. As you may have guess, the button in the bottom is to switch back  to previous conversion mode.


    In any of those previous scenarios, the arrow on the left allows you to erase the last symbol selected. And also you can navigate to the Help section, by pressing the “i” on the right.


    Using the Simple view, you can only represent numbers from 1 to 3,999. Trying to enter a number out of those bounds is not supported. If you want to convert numbers greater than 3,999 you should try the Pro View.
